Tennis Players (Hard)

A certain tennis academy has \(101\) members. To prepare for the US-Open, the math-loving instuctors of the academy have the members play until the following rule holds true: for every possible group of \(50\) players, there is a player outside that group who has played against everyone within it. Prove that there is at least one player who has played against everyone in the academy.
